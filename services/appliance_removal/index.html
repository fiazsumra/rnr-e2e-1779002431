Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to disconnect the appliance before you arrive?
No, we handle disconnection for most appliances. We'll unhook washers, dryers, and dishwashers as part of the service. For gas ranges or dryers, we recommend having a licensed plumber disconnect the gas line beforehand for safety, though we can coordinate that if needed.
What do you do with the appliances you remove?
Working appliances in decent condition are donated to local charities when possible. Units that can't be reused are taken to certified recycling facilities where metals, plastics, and other materials are recovered. Refrigerators and AC units go through proper Freon recovery to meet EPA requirements.
Can you remove built-in appliances?
Yes, we remove built-in dishwashers, microwaves, and ovens. These jobs sometimes take a bit longer because we need to carefully detach cabinetry panels and trim, but we handle it carefully to avoid damaging your kitchen.
How quickly can you schedule a pickup?
We offer same-day and next-day service across most of the Upstate depending on our schedule. If you're coordinating with a delivery or installation, let us know the timing and we'll do our best to accommodate.
Do you handle commercial or restaurant equipment?
Yes, we remove commercial refrigerators, ovens, dishwashers, and other restaurant equipment. These jobs typically require custom quotes because commercial units are heavier and may need special disposal.
What if my appliance is in a basement or upstairs?
We remove appliances from any floor of your home. Stairs and tight spaces may add to the time and effort required, which can affect pricing for especially heavy items, but we'll give you an honest quote upfront and protect your walls and floors during the move.
